Project Overview

In 2045, Indonesia enters a pivotal era dubbed its 'golden generation,' characterized by a demographic advantage with a larger working-age population. To leverage this opportunity and advance towards Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), effective education and career planning are essential.

Inspired by this need, I developed a mobile platform tailored to address challenges in education and career decision-making. From conceptualization to implementation, I crafted the app from scratch, accompanied by a comprehensive design system and documentation to ensure its longevity and impact.

Problem Statement

To prepare for this "golden generation," it's crucial for youth to have quality education and strong career understanding. Currently, many students feel mismatched with their majors, with 87% expressing dissatisfaction. Lack of career readiness, due to low self-confidence (83%) and negative self-perception (80%), hinders progress. This directly impacts Quality Education(SDG 4), Decent Work and Economic Growth(SDG8), serta Partnerships for The Goals(SDG 17).

Solution

Introducing the "What's next?" app: It offers personalized major suggestions through MBTI tests, recommends accredited schools, enables chat consultations, provides 1-on-1 mentoring with professionals, hosts skill training bootcamps, and offers additional resources for exploring careers. It's a comprehensive platform empowering Indonesian youth to navigate their education and careers effectively.

MBTI Test

The MBTI test helps users identify majors that match their personality. They can take and retake the test to ensure their decisions align with their personality traits.

Mentoring 1 on 1

Users can engage in 1-on-1 mentoring with field experts. This personalized guidance offers in-depth insights, valuable advice, and industry contacts. With expert mentorship, users confidently plan career steps, identify opportunities, and develop effective strategies.

Bootcamp

The bootcamp is designed to comprehensively train participants, focusing on both soft skills and hard skills. This ensures that with the necessary interpersonal and technical abilities to succeed in their careers.

My Journey

Track user MBTI tests and bootcamp participation to effortlessly monitor their skill improvement journey.

College Suggestions

Offer tailored major recommendations aligned with accredited programs for optimal educational outcomes.

Process

Research

Phase 01

User Interview

I conducted interviews with four individuals, comprising two students who are grappling with the decision of selecting a major, and two others who are feeling apprehensive and uncertain about charting their future career paths

Key takeaways

  • → Most people struggle to find reliable study and career information
  • → Respondents frequently lean on each other for support in managing anxiety and depression tied to their study and career aspirations
  • → Most respondents struggle with decision-making due to numerous choices and a lack of guidance resources
  • → Most respondents feel depressed about choosing their study and career paths because they doubt their skills and mindset

Define

Phase 02

User Persona

The user analysis of Persona indicates that the main users of the "What's next?" app are students uncertain about their career paths or future studies. They need accurate information, expert consultations, and skill enhancement programs relevant to industry demands.

Ideate

Phase 03

How might we?

After understanding the user's problem statement and pain points, I use "How Might We" (HMW) questions to spark creative ideas and solutions. This approach helps generate innovative solutions that can be applied in the application, making it easier to address the users' challenges effectively.

Problem-Solving Questions

  • → How might we help users find reliable information about their career and study options?
  • → How might we reduce the anxiety and depression users feel when making decisions about their future?
  • → How might we provide a platform for users to develop both hard and soft skills needed for their careers?
  • → How might we offer personalized mentoring to assist users in their career and study choices?

Testing

Phase 06

Usability Testing

The usability testing will target five representative users, consisting of students or individuals with similar backgrounds and members of the general public. These users have expressed difficulties in determining their future studies and career paths. The testing will employ the System Usability Scale (SUS) method to evaluate the system's effectiveness

Based on the usability testing results, I calculated the SUS scores and found that all users successfully completed the tasks, rating the system from above neutral to strongly agree (scores between 5 and 10). However, some tasks posed challenges. For example, task 6 in the bootcamp feature had a 51.7% misclick rate and took an average of 15.6 seconds. Users suggested adding more details to this feature. Additionally, task 4 of the MBTI test feature had a 54.1% misclick rate and took 46.8 seconds, which is expected given the complexity of the questions.

The usability testing resulted in a SUS score of 94.6%, indicating that the UX design of "What's Next?" is highly acceptable. Users liked the application and felt it effectively solved their problems, making them likely to recommend it to others.

Iteration

From the testing and feedback provided by users, we have identified several areas for improvement. These improvements address the issues encountered during usability testing and incorporate user suggestions to enhance the overall experience. Below are the detailed results and changes made based on what was tested

1. Enhanced Search Functionality Before: Users had only one search field with no clear categories.
After: Now, popular search categories like "Business," "Marketing," and "IT Support" have been incorporated.

Usability Boost: This update streamlines the search process, allowing users to quickly locate courses relevant to their interests or needs.

2. Detailed Course Information Display Before: The course detail page lacked a direct example.
After: Users now enjoy a comprehensive course detail page featuring ratings, concise descriptions, and additional information.

Usability Boost: By offering more insights upfront, users can make informed decisions about enrolling, fostering trust and satisfaction.

3. Enhanced Navigation Experience Before: The interface was basic with limited navigation features.
After: We've introduced navigation enhancements such as search categories and a richer detail page design.

Usability Boost: With an improved interface and intuitive navigation, users can effortlessly find and access the information they need, enhancing their overall experience.

Reflection

Phase 06

What are the next steps?

I've realized that some parts of the app design are based on my assumptions rather than solid data. I'm focused on improving this by creating a more organized and consistent design system for both components and user flows. I've noticed that I'm spending too much time on elements outside of this system and still haven't nailed down the best way for users to navigate the app. Plus, I know I haven't fully considered the user experience. I'm committed to diving deeper and innovating to craft a fresh user experience for our study and career solution app

This project has been a great learning experience for me, especially diving into UI/UX for the first time. It felt like an exciting journey, introducing me to various roles like UX researcher, UX writer, and finally UI designer. Through this process, I gained a comprehensive understanding of UI/UX design.

Despite encountering challenges, I found them enjoyable. Working with the design system highlighted the importance of effective planning and organization. Usability testing sessions revealed insights I hadn't considered before, enhancing my problem-solving skills and understanding of decision-making urgency for different individuals. Overall, this experience significantly improved my design skills.
