Project Overview

This regional government website streamlines administrative procedures for residents, including the issuance of ID cards, birth certificates, and family cards. It also provides comprehensive information and updates on local administrative processes, ensuring residents have easy access to the latest news and guidelines related to population administration.

Why this project?

During my internship at the SKH Population and Civil Registry Office, I was assigned to redesign their official website to make it more minimalist and up-to-date. I discovered that the existing site had an outdated design, poorly organized information, and users frequently complained about the difficulty of finding relevant information and the overall unattractive appearance of the site.

Some issues that I discovered

  • Outdated visual design that fails to reflect the local government's commitment to being fast, easy, and accurate
  • Poorly organized content, making it difficult for users to find necessary information
  • Chaotic visual layout and lack of visual hierarchy, making it hard to distinguish important sections
  • Insufficient details on local government services and benefits, leaving potential users unclear about the value provided

Solution

Introducing the "What's next?" app: It offers personalized major suggestions through MBTI tests, recommends accredited schools, enables chat consultations, provides 1-on-1 mentoring with professionals, hosts skill training bootcamps, and offers additional resources for exploring careers. It's a comprehensive platform empowering Indonesian youth to navigate their education and careers effectively.

Phase 06

What are the next steps?

I've realized that some parts of the app design are based on my assumptions rather than solid data. I'm focused on improving this by creating a more organized and consistent design system for both components and user flows. I've noticed that I'm spending too much time on elements outside of this system and still haven't nailed down the best way for users to navigate the app. Plus, I know I haven't fully considered the user experience. I'm committed to diving deeper and innovating to craft a fresh user experience for our study and career solution app

This project has been a great learning experience for me, especially diving into UI/UX for the first time. It felt like an exciting journey, introducing me to various roles like UX researcher, UX writer, and finally UI designer. Through this process, I gained a comprehensive understanding of UI/UX design.

Despite encountering challenges, I found them enjoyable. Working with the design system highlighted the importance of effective planning and organization. Usability testing sessions revealed insights I hadn't considered before, enhancing my problem-solving skills and understanding of decision-making urgency for different individuals. Overall, this experience significantly improved my design skills.
